Professional Summary

Supply chain thinking.
Data engineering precision.

I chose to build my career around data because I believe the most valuable business decisions are driven by evidence, not assumptions. My MS in Data Science strengthened my skills in analytics, machine learning, statistical modeling, and AI, while my professional experience allowed me to apply these concepts to supply chain optimization, business intelligence, and operational analytics. I enjoy bridging the gap between raw data and strategic decisions by building scalable analytical solutions that deliver real impact.

Early in my career as a Supply Chain Analyst at IFFCO Pakistan, a large FMCG conglomerate, I built KPI dashboards tracking work-in-progress, lead times, inventory turns, and supplier performance — and used that visibility to drive a 30% reduction in WIP and a 15% reduction in average lead time. That experience shaped how I think about every dataset since: not "what does this number say," but "what supply chain or business decision does this number need to support?"

I carried that same decision-support mindset into Business Intelligence and Data Analytics roles in pharma and logistics — building Power BI platforms, automating reporting that used to take two days, and establishing fleet, route, and on-time-delivery KPIs at one of Pakistan's largest logistics groups. Along the way I picked up the statistical and forecasting backbone (SARIMA/ARIMA time series, predictive analytics, an MS in Data Science from NED) to move from descriptive dashboards to forward-looking forecasts.

None of that works without solid Data Engineering underneath it. I design ETL/ELT pipelines, model data warehouses, automate validation and reconciliation, and keep production PostgreSQL/MySQL systems clean — the technical backbone that makes the analytics and supply chain work trustworthy. Today, at Afiniti, I'm applying that same rigor to pipelines that feed live AI systems, and exploring what it means to make data genuinely "AI-ready" for agentic systems.

If you're looking for someone who can sit in the supply chain conversation, the analytics conversation, and the engineering conversation — and connect all three to a business outcome — that's the problem I show up for.
