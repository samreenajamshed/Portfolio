About Me

Engineering background.
Data obsession.

I came into data engineering the long way — through an Electrical Engineering degree at NED University, where I spent a lot of time thinking about systems, signals, and how things connect. What I didn't expect was that the most interesting "signals" I'd ever work with would be business data: messy, inconsistent, full of hidden patterns waiting to be useful.

After my BS, I started working as a Data Analyst and quickly realized that the gap between "having data" and "making decisions with data" is enormous. Companies were sitting on months of records they couldn't query, dashboards nobody trusted, and reports that took two days to produce manually. That frustration pushed me toward engineering — not just analyzing data, but building the infrastructure that makes analysis possible.

Over the last 6 years, I've worked across supply chain, pharmaceuticals, logistics, and AI-driven telecoms. I've built ETL pipelines in Airflow, designed KPI dashboards that actually got used, automated reporting workflows that saved teams hours every week, and led analytics functions across multiple business units. My MS in Data Science from NED gave me the statistical backbone — SARIMA, forecasting, time series — but the real learning happened in production, under deadline, when the pipeline breaks at 2 AM.

Right now I'm genuinely excited about Agentic AI. Not as a buzzword, but as a real shift in how data systems will work — where pipelines don't just move data, they reason about it. I've been preparing data frameworks for AI readiness and thinking carefully about what it means to build data infrastructure that an LLM can actually work with. That's the next chapter I want to be part of.

If you're building a data platform that needs to be reliable, documented, and actually understood by the business — that's the kind of problem I show up for.
